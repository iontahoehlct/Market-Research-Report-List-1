<p><strong>The Edible Hydrogenated Oils Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Partially Hydrogenated Oils</li><li>Fully Hydrogenated Oils</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Edible hydrogenated oils are classified into partially hydrogenated and fully hydrogenated oils based on the degree of hydrogenation they undergo. Partially hydrogenated oils contain trans fats, which can enhance flavor and shelf life but pose health risks. These oils are commonly used in processed foods. Fully hydrogenated oils, on the other hand, have no trans fats, resulting in a more stable product with a neutral flavor. They’re often used in margarines and cooking oils as healthier alternatives to partially hydrogenated oils.</p></p>
